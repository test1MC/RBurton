Managing shipping settings

Use the Shipping Settings section to define how your site calculates shipping, set free shipping thresholds (if relevant), designate which shipping options to offer your customers, and enter your carrier settings. Use the Shipping Settings area to determine how you will ship your products, and how you will charge your customers for shipping.

Before configuring these settings, be certain you have the appropriate accounts set up with the shipping carriers you want to use on your site.

There are certain website settings that will affect shipping settings. By default, subscriptions receive free shipping. Enable Charge Shipping on Subscriptions at Users > Website Settings > Payments > Subscription Settings if you want to charge customers for the cost of shipping their subscription order.

There are also free shipping promotion settings at Users > Website Settings > Site Layout > Shopping Cart Settings.

To learn about obtaining accounts and configuring various shippers with your site, refer to the following knowledge base articles.

  • FedEx - Refer to FedEx Web Services Integration knowledge base article #1717. View the FedEx International Express Countries Served via US export and US import. For example, FedEx does not ship to: Greece, Kosovo, Latvia, Mexico, Spain, Thailand, or Turkey.
  • USPS - Refer to USPS and Endicia Shipping Accounts knowledge base article #2165
  • UPS - Refer to Setting up UPS knowledge base article #1951. UPS Standard falls under the category of international shipping.
  • UPS Mail Innovations® - Refer to Setting up UPS Mail Innovations® knowledge base article #2065.

Managing shipping types

In the Shipping Type area, you can control the type of shipping used by your site. Your site will only use only one of the six shipping types explained below. Only the Calculated and/or flat rates (multiple carriers) type will return rates from multiple shipping providers. The other five shipping settings are each limited to only one shipping carrier.

Configure shipping providers in Express Shipping Carrier Settings. You only need to set up one type:

Shipping type Description
Calculated rates (one carrier) All order shipping cost rates displayed to customers are calculated via rates returned from the one shipping carrier selected, for example, FedEx, UPS, and so on. The rates calculated are based on the credentials entered under Express Shipping Rates, Tracking and Label Processing Settings. If no credentials are entered, the default Aptos credentials are passed on to retrieve rates.
10% of shopping card (one carrier) Ground order shipping cost is 10% of the customer’s shopping cart sub-total. Returns rates from only one shipping carrier.
Calculated and/or flat rates (multiple carriers) This shipping type is very flexible as it allows for multiple carriers, range groups, calculated shipping rates, and provides rates based off weight or cart total. You can set up all shipping rates and allow for rate markups or markdowns. You can also set up shipping regions to help fine-tune your shipping costs.
Calculated and/or flat rates (one carrier) Ground order shipping cost is based on a range set up on this page. Returns rates from only one shipping carrier.
Free Ground Shipping (one carrier) For orders shipped via ground, the shipping cost is always free. Returns rates from only one shipping carrier.
Product-specific rates (one carrier) Ground order shipping cost is the sum of the shipping cost of the items in customer’s shopping cart. Returns rates from only one shipping carrier. Product-specific shipping rates are configured at the Domestic Fixed Rate and Int'l Fixed Rate fields in Content > Products > Add/Edit > [Find Product] > Details > Shipping > Shipping Details.

 Calculated and/or flat rates (multiple carriers)

The Calculated and/or flat rates (multiple carriers) setting helps you provide the best shipping rates to your customers and helps to ensure that the rates you provide can cover your costs. This shipping setting allows for multiple carriers and calculated shipping and provides rates based off weight or cart total. It also gives you the ability to have an additional pricing markup for each subsequent product added to the cart. This allows for more flexibility when providing shipping rates to your customers. You can also select shipping regions to be used for each price tier. Shipping regions are configured on the Commerce > Shipping > Regions page.

If more than one rate for a given shipping service can be returned for a customer, the platform will return the cheapest rate for that shipping service.

For example, if you have configured the following for UPS Ground:

  • Cart subtotal between $0.01 and $25 = shipping rate of $5
  • Cart subtotal between $25 and $50 = shipping rate of $8

If the customer's cart subtotal is exactly $25, which means both of the shipping rates above could be applied, the system returns the cheaper rate, which is $5.

If you are using FedEx SmartPost on your site, you must add a tier for SmartPost rates to be returned based on your business needs and for all other options. In addition there are other shipping settings that need to be set up.

Do Not Make Live Calls to Shipping Carriers

If your site is configured to use "Calculated and/or flat rates (multiple carriers)" and each rate is a "flat rate" (as opposed to calculated) and assigned to a geographic region, you can enable this setting to bypass the call to shipping carriers.

This can improve the performance of the checkout page; however, it means that the availability of the configured shipping services will not be verified.

To enable this feature, the shipping region must be selected for each and every pricing range and the rate type must be flat. Only then will the setting be selectable and the platform won't call the shipping carriers to retrieve shipping services/rates.

Dimensional Add-On (inches/centimeters)

This setting overrides the default box size and default box weight settings (above).

If one box in the order, it uses the box dimensions entered at the product level.

For each box added to the order, the system adds the values set here to the total shipping box size for the order.

Calculate Tiered Freight on products after grouping:

When calculating shipping rates for Custom Freight when Tiered Freight is available, you can configure shipping to add the weight of all the products in the same group and calculate freight based on the sum of the weights. Enabling this overrides the system calculating each product separately then adding the sum of the dollar values for the shipping cost.

There may be a tier set up to cost $17.50 for 0-20 pounds, $35.00 for 21-40 pounds and $52.50 for 41-60 pounds. If you have two products in this tier (Product A and Product B) both products may weigh 10 pounds. If this option is not selected and a customer orders 3 of Product A and 1 of Product B, the customer is charged $52.50 for shipping since Product A falls under 21-40 pounds and Product B falls under 0-20 pounds.

If the option is enabled and a customer orders 3 of Product A and 1 of Product B, they are charged $35 for shipping since they have 4 products in the tier at a weight of 10 pounds each.
