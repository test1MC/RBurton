About the Content tab

Use the EOM site manager Content tab to create, organize, and manage all textual and visual information about your business, products, and service offerings on your website.

You can manage: site taxonomy; create, edit, and manage products; create blogs; and much more.

Use this tab to optimize content that your customers, and search engines, see.

Menu definitions and descriptions

Here's a list of definitions for the categorization hierarchy typically used in an EOM deployment.

Menu item Definition
Categories Categories are the highest level of classification for your products. In a brick and mortar store, think of this as the general overhead sign pointing you to a general department (i.e. outerwear, plumbing, frozen foods). Some ecommerce sites with less complex product sets will not need to distinguish by Category. When setting up these ecommerce stores, you can skip straight to Sub-categories and products.
Sub-Categories This is the second tier of classifications (taxonomy) for your products. If Category is the general overhead sign in a store, Sub-Category would be the more detailed listing beneath the sign. For example, if Frozen Food is the Category, the Sub-Categories could be: pizza, vegetables, and/or frozen dinners. Sub-Categories can either always appear expanded, or collapsed and expandable as part of your home page menu.
Classifications Classifications further organize your Sub-Category into specific products. Included in this subset are product attributes like: price or material.
Brands A Brand contains products across all Categories and Sub-Categories and is grouped by a manufacturer. If all brands are the same, you don’t have to display this in your store. However, when setting up products in the site manager, you will still need to associate products with a brand/manufacturer.
Import/Export This option allows you to bulk import products without options, or edit existing ones using the Aptos pre-formatted spreadsheet. You should always do an export to ensure you are using the most recent spreadsheet version. This will also guide you on the proper format for each column and row, as well as which field is required.
Products Very simply put, Products are the items in your store. You will set up your products after you have set up all other Categories. This will allow you to associate the individual products with Brands, Categories, Sub-Categories, and so on.
Blogs In the Blogs tab you can create, manage, and publish blog content. Blogging is an exceptional way to enhance your SEOsearch engine optimization because you are adding fresh content to your site.
Locations If your products are also available in physical stores you can enter store locations in the Locations tab.
Pages You can edit existing pages, or create new ones from scratch in the Pages tab. Examples of pages are: email templates, click-to-brick, 301 redirects, master pages, and so on.
Customers The Customers tab is your communication portal to existing or future consumers.
International Aptos has created a number of tools to help you define who your international customers are and allows you to translate key phrases throughout your site for easier accessibility.
Tasks In Tasks you can assign tasks to specific team members such as: to fulfill orders, manage customer requests, and more.
