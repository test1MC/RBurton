Average cost calculation

Average cost is determined by dividing the total on hand cost by the total on hand units.

If calculating the average cost at the style level, then the on-hand cost and units for all SKUs belonging to a style are totaled at either the location, jurisdiction or chain level. In this case, all SKUs belonging to that style are posted with the same average cost.

If calculating the average cost at the SKU level, then the system will only total the on-hand cost and units for a SKU, resulting in a different average cost by SKU. As is the case with the style average cost, it will total the SKU on-hand cost and units at the location, jurisdiction or chain level.

Average cost levels

Depending on the system parameters for average cost levels, average cost can be calculated at these levels:

Style/chain

The style/chain average cost is determined by dividing the style/chain total on hand cost by the style/chain total on hand units. The on-hand cost and on-hand units for all locations across the chain (and across all jurisdictions) are summed up by style. Chain average costs are calculated in the home currency using home costs and are converted to location currency when posting to location costs using the domestic equivalent rate of the location currency

 Style/jurisdiction

The style/jurisdiction average cost is determined by dividing the style's total on-hand cost by the style's total on-hand units for the jurisdiction. The on-hand cost and on-hand units for all locations that belong to the jurisdiction are summed up by style.

 Style/location

The style/location average cost is determined by dividing the style's total OH cost by the style total OH units for the location. The on-hand cost and on-hand units for only the location are summed up by style.

 SKU/jurisdiction

The SKU/jurisdiction average cost is determined by dividing the SKU's total on-hand cost by the SKU's total on-hand units for the jurisdiction. The on-hand cost and on-hand units for all locations that belong to the jurisdiction are summed up by SKU.

 SKU/location

The SKU/location average cost is determined by dividing the SKU's total OH cost by the SKU total OH units for the location. The on-hand cost and on-hand units for only the location are summed up by SKU.

If SKU average cost cannot be calculated for the reasons listed below, then the style's average cost will be used.

Either or both the total OH cost or total OH units for the SKU is negative

There are no transactions in IB Inventory for that SKU in the entire chain (i.e. style has not been received in the chain).

There are no transactions in IB Inventory for that SKU and for the jurisdiction that the average cost is needed for (i.e. style has not been received in that jurisdiction). Applicable only when average cost location level parameter is Jurisdiction.

There are no transactions in IB Inventory for that SKU and for the location that the average cost is needed for (i.e. style has not been received in that location). Applicable only when average cost location level parameter is location.

If the style/location average cost cannot be calculated for the reasons listed below, then the style's last net final cost will be used as the average cost

Either or both the total OH cost or total OH units for the style is negative

There are no transactions in IB Inventory for that style in the entire chain (i.e. style has not been received in the chain).

There are no transactions in IB Inventory for that style and for the jurisdiction that the average cost is needed for (i.e. style has not been received in that jurisdiction). Applicable only when average cost location level parameter is Jurisdiction.

There are no transactions in IB Inventory for that style and for the location that the average cost is needed for (i.e. style has not been received in that location). Applicable only when average cost location level parameter is location.

If the style average cost (regardless of the location level) cannot be calculated for the reasons listed above and that style does not have a last net final cost, then the style's goal IMU% (from its merchandise group characteristics) will be used as the average cost.

Rounding takes place on the result of the average cost calculation. It is rounded to two decimal places; values greater than or equal to 0.995 are rounded up to 1.00, and values less than 0.995 are rounded down to 0.99.
