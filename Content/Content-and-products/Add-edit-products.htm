Adding and editing products

Use the Add/Edit area of the Product menu to add a new product in the Site Manager. You can also define and edit the details of your products here.

Adding products

Before you begin adding products in the Site Manager, you first need to set up and/or define your Categories, Sub-Categories, Brands, and understand how to set up the hierarchy/taxonomy of your site down to the product level. You also need to define the Classifications and Comparable Specifications, if applicable to your site. You need to understand and plan meta data and search engine submissions.

To add products
  1. In Content, select Products > Add/Edit.

  2. Click the Add button (+) to open the Add New Product window.

  3. Select the Brand and Sub-Category to which the product belongs from the lists.

  4. Enter the Display Name of the product as you want it to appear on your website.

  5. In the Part # field, enter the part number, style number, manufacturer’s part number, stock number, or any other uniquely identifying number when adding a product if you choose to do so. This field may need to be left blank, especially in the case where the Part/Style# (SKU) is stored at the option level.

  6. If relevant, enter the MSRP associated with the product.

    Depending on the wiring of your site, this may or may not appear on your website.

  7. In Cost, enter your cost for the product.

  8. In Price, enter the price that the product will sell for on your site. This is the value that populates in the product's "Sell ForNote, this is not the same as the "On Sale" price." field. Note that you can also set the price can as a margin of cost. To do that instead:

    1. Click on the Set From Margin link. A small pop-up window opens.

    2. Enter the margin you want to use for setting the Sell For price of this product. In this example, 20% was added.

    3. Click OK to continue.

  9. If you are having a sale you can enter the sale price in the On Sale Price field when adding a product. Typically, the Price appears with a strike-through on your site, and the On Sale Price appears beside it.

  10. Choose check box Options as needed:

    1. Active – If you don't want the product entered with an active status at this time, de-select the Active checkbox.

    2. This is an Aptos electronic gift card – Select this box if you want to use e-gift card functionality.

      This is not a physical gift card which is configured as a product.

    3. This product is a donation that will appear at checkout – This product is a digital good and only appears on the final checkout page. Your customers will be given the ability to donate a specific amount or round their order up to the nearest dollar. You can also display multiple charities at checkout.

  11. Click the Save button at the bottom of this window to save the new product.
 Edit Product Details

The Product Details view is where you control all the details of your product(s). You can create, view, and edit all the details of each product here. All of the information that you have populated in Classifications, Comparable Specifications, Product Options, and so on, come into play in this section.

Work through each tab and section of the Product page, making changes as needed.

Be sure to Save often.
To search for products
  1. In Content, select Products > Add/Edit.
  2. Use the search field to filter by Product Name, Part #, Alternate Part # or UPC. You can also narrow your search with the options:

    1. Sub-Category – Select the product's sub-category from the list, or select Uncategorized Products to view products assigned to sub- categories that have been deleted.

    2. Include Shared Products – Select this box to include products that have been shared in the selected sub-category.

    3. Product Status – You can choose to search by Inventory Status.You can narrow your search by Archived Product, Discontinued Product, Incomplete Product, or Live Product.

    4. Inventory Status – You can choose to search by Inventory Status.

    5. Quantity – Narrow your search by inventory quantity, if desired.

    6. Brand – You can search by product brand.

    7. Supplier – You can search by product supplier; useful if you only want to work with products from a particular supplier.

      1. Check box Options – You can also choose to filter your search by any or all of:

        1. Show Deleted - filter to show deleted products.

        2. On Sale - filters for products that are on sale.

        3. Show Inactive - filters to show inactive products.

        4. Missing Image - filters to show only the products that are missing the main product image.

  3. Click Search, and products matching your search criteria are displayed on the Product page.

The Product Details window contains five tabs in which you can control all aspects of the product. Each tab contains an array of panels, labeled with a black bar. Most of the panels in the window are collapsed to make viewing easier. Click on a bar to expand and/or collapse each panel.

To set up a payment plan

When setting up a payment plan, first determine the product's down payment amount, the number of payments, and the time interval for payment processing.

This example outlines the settings for a product with a down payment price of $10, with a payment plan of 3 monthly payments of $30 each.

  1. ​Set the Sell For price in the Price Details area with the product's down payment amount. In the example, the down payment amount is set to 10.00.
  2. In the Payment Plan area, set the Payment Amount to the amount to be collected with each payment. In this example, the payment amount is set to 30.00.
  3. Enter the total number of future payments to be collected from the # of Payments list menu. This is only the future payments and does not include the payment received at the time of checkout. The example illustrated here is set to receive 3 more payments.

 Pricing tab—Subscriptions panel

Use subscriptions to create recurring revenue streams from your customers. Keep in mind that payment for subscriptions must be with credit cards, cash/check/money order, or PayPal.

Subscriptions can also be applied to bundled products.

Your site must be designed and wired to accommodate subscriptions. If it is, you can use this section to define the terms of the product's recurring shipments.
Subscriptions panel fields
Field Description
Subscription Product to Purchase:

This section allows you to assign what products you want the customer to receive on a recurring basis.

  • If the product is the same one you are editing, select the Purchase Current Product check box.
  • If you want to assign a different product, use the Select Different Product list menu to choose the one you want. This menu is populated with Cross-Sell groups configured in Content > Sub- Categories > Cross-Sell/Up-Sell. An example of a cross-sell/up-sell item might be a "Fruit of the Month" club which offers a different fruit basket every month depending on the season.
Default Subscription Period: Enter the subscription interval in this field. If the product is to be shipped every 30 days, enter 30 and choose Days from the list menu. You can also choose intervals of weeks, months, and years.
Additional Selectable Periods (Optional): You can customize the intervals by adding them in this field. For example, if a customer only wants to receive a product once every 60 days, add a comma-delimited list such as: 30,60,120 in this field. If your site is wired for it, the customer will then be able to choose from one of these interval options.
Add a New Subscription Rule:

In this area, which must be completed for subscriptions, define the quantity to ship per subscription and the price to charge the customer each month for the subscription. You can also change the auto-renew settings to re-price a product. An example of a way to use this field to promote customer loyalty, would be to set it to give away items at certain intervals.

  • •Quantity: This defines the quantity of the product to be shipped per subscription.
  • Value: The price that will be charged for the order. The prices are further defined in the Type field.
  • Type: Choose whether the Value (price) defined above, is a set price, a fixed adjustment, or a percentage.
    • Set Price -When set price is selected, when a product becomes part of the subscription, this is the subscription price defined in the Value field.
    • Fixed Adjustment - When selected, the product becoming part of the subscription will have its price adjusted by the amount specified in the Value field.
    • Percentage - If percentage is selected, the product becoming part of the subscription will have its price adjusted by the percentage (%) specified in the Value field.
  • Repeat Interval: Defines when the subscription rule applies. If the rule should apply to every subscription order, set the Repeat Interval to 1. If the rule should apply to every 10th subscription order, set the Repeat Interval to 10. If there is an interval conflict, where two rules could apply to an order, the rule with the higher Repeat Interval value will be applied. For example, if you set up a rule with a Repeat Interval of '1' and another rule with a Repeat Interval of '10', the rule with '10' will apply to the 10th subscription order.
  • Tier Group (Optional): If this subscription is for a specific tier group, select it from the list menu.

Bundle Options

In EOM you can create a product that is made up of multiple products and sold as a package. The best way to do this is to bundle the products together and create a new product. For example, you would use this feature to offer a bundle of products that consists of several products at a pre-defined price, commonly, at a discounted price.

If the item price is marked down for each item in the bundle to reach the total bundle price. So if three products you want to include in a bundle are $10 each, you can bundle these products and offer them at a bundled price of $21 which makes the price of each product in the bundle now only $7.

In the Bundle Options area, make your selections.

Yes, this item is a bundled package of multiple products. Add all the products that you will be selling together as a package unit. To set this feature up, checkmark this box. This will allow you to select the Sub-Category from which to create the bundled products.

Yes, all items in this bundle must ship together. There may be times when you want all items in a product bundle to ship together. If so, checkmark this rule. With this rule in place, if even one item in the bundle is on backorder, all items in the bundle will be placed in the backordered status until all items are available to ship. This new 'Must Ship Together' rule applies to both product bundles and cross-sell groups set to 'Must Ship Together'. This rule is considered before the order is created and placed into the system.

Add New Item(s) to Bundle - Select the Sub-Category for the products associated in the bundle. Once you select a Sub-Category, a drop-down menu will appear with a list of all the products within that Sub-Category from which you can begin making your selections. The selected items will display on the screen. If you want the bundle to consist of more products, simply duplicate these steps. This creates a bundle "shell" product so this group of products can live together on the front end of your site.

  • Quantity - If the bundle consists of a quantity of these items, select the quantity of the item in the Quantity field.

  • Price - After you have selected the products that will be in the bundle, you can set the price at the item level for all the items that make up a bundle. The price entered here is for visual use only as it will display on the order confirmation page and email, as well as in Site Manager when a bundle purchase is made. (The bundle item price does not need to match the individual product price.) The price that the customer actually pays is the price set for the master bundle product. You still need to set the master bundle product price for the master bundle item.

    The pricing for all items in each bundle should add up to the master bundle item price. This will cause much less confusion for your customers.

  • Main Item - Choose whether you want to set a product as the main item.

    • Yes - If you select a bundle item as the main item then the price associated to it will be the master bundle price. No more than one product may be defined as the main item. This is so that the items will have prices associated with them on the invoice. Even with a main item defined, you can still assign pricing to the other items that make up the bundle, however this could be confusing to your customers during order confirmation. Inventory of "child" products (i.e, accessories) does not get decremented.

    • No - If you do not select a bundle item as the main bundle item then the price column will be blank unless you populate each individual price field, as described above. On the order confirmation page, order confirmation email, edit order screen, and elsewhere on your site an order can be viewed, only the items in the bundle will be shown, not the shell product.

    For accounting purposes, the price assigned to each item that makes up the bundle is recorded and tied to the order should you need to reference the discounted price.
    If you select an item as a main item, no more than one product can be defined as the main item. This is so that the items will have prices associated with them on the invoice. Inventory of "child" products (for example, accessories) does not get decremented.
