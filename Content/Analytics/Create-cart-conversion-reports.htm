Creating Cart Conversion reports

Use the Cart Conversion report to identify and monitor abandoned carts. You can then send an email to the visitors who left your site with items still in their carts, and offer them an incentive for returning and purchasing those items.

Note: Some statistics show an average of 70% shopping cart abandonment rates in ecommerce. This can vary depending on your business’s vertical. For strategies on how to improve your shopping cart conversion rates, contact your Client Manager.

The report displays a bar graph showing summary data on Site Visitors, Real People, Abandoned Carts, Orders, and Shopping Carts. In addition, a table showing shopping cart conversion performance is displayed.

Summary information displayed includes:

  • Site Visitors; reflects all affiliate hits by spiders, bots crawling on the site, and so on.

  • Real People; includes only the actual number of affiliate hits by people who visited your site.
  • Abandoned Carts
  • Orders
  • Shopping Carts
  • % of Visits turned into Carts
  • % of Visits turned into Orders
  • % of Carts turned into Orders
  • % of Carts abandoned

Report details

In addition to the Summary Information at the top of the page, as you scroll down the page you will see:

  • Abandonment Details (by Page, IP Address, Affiliate, Browser, and Referring URL).
  • Daily Cart Metrics for the date range selected, and export the file to Excel, if desired.
  • Outstanding Carts (you can choose to only show carts with customer data). You can export the file to Excel and/or send an email to these customers.
  • Outstanding Carts with Customer Data and without Orders in 72 hours. You can export the file to Excel and/or send an email to these customers.

To create a Cart Conversion report

  1. In Analytics, selectTraffic Reports > Cart Conversion.
  3. In the Affiliate list, select one or All Affiliates.

  4. Select whether to Include Detailed Cart Information.

    Note: Checking this option also generates the Abandoned Carts section of the report. From this section you can choose to email owners of abandoned carts.

  5. Click the Search button .

  6. To export any of those areas that support it to an Excel spreadsheet for reporting or printing, click itsExport to Excel button .

  7. To email customers who have submitted customer data, but who left your site without purchasing the items in their cart, scroll to the Abandoned Cart area of the report, and click the Email button. This takes you to the Marketing > Email > Create page where you can create and send a promotion or other email to encourage them to complete the purchase process.

    Note: The Abandoned Cart area of the report is generated only if you enable the Include Detailed Cart Information option before clicking Search.
